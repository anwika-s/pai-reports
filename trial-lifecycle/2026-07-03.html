8Data limits (stated plainly)

  • Credits: is_users.credits = 0 for all 1,421 matched users — the cohort is defined by trial exhaustion, so the 0–100% usage distribution collapses to "~100% consumed." A real allocation-vs-consumption curve needs an activation-based cohort.
  • Retention D1/D3/D7: the events table has no sessionId column, so true session-return curves aren't available. Proxy: avg 4.29 active days/user.
  • 26 of 1,447 cohort users have no is_users row (deleted/ID mismatch); MySQL-derived cuts use the 1,421 matched.
  • "7-day / 500-credit" naming: no such package exists (Starter/TRIAL is catalogued 60-day / 5,000-credit). Report is against the behavioural trial-expiry cohort. Trial length observed 9–22 days, not a clean 7.
  • n = 13 genuine upgraders — every segmentation (plan, country, device) is directional only; cohort-level numbers (1,447 base, funnel, engagement) are solid.

9Recommendations

  • Fix checkout, not awareness. 616 open checkout, 13 pay (98% abandon). A/B the payment step (fewer fields, local payment methods for GCC/EU). This is the single biggest lever.
  • Rescue the 709 who never made a deck. Front-load a "first deck in 60s" onboarding — the expiry gate is wasted on users who never saw value.
  • Convert at expiry, deliberately. Genuine upgrades land ~15 days out, at the gate. Target the 341 highly-engaged non-converters with an expiry offer (discount / extension).
  • Investigate the lapsed-Pro cyclers. 10 former Pro subscribers dropped to Starter and hit the trial again — a win-back/reactivation campaign, distinct from new-trial conversion.
  • Zero Gold traction — confirm Gold is even surfaced in the trial upsell path.
