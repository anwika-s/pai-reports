How the numbers were built & what to trust

  • Source of truth: "Paid" comes from MySQL is_stripepayments (every row is a real captured payment — no failed/refund rows), not the ClickHouse payment_flow_end event, which under-counts by ~30%.
  • Join: ClickHouse userId → is_users.id → is_stripepayments.userid — direct numeric, no email/casting ambiguity.
  • Attribution: a payment counts as a conversion only if it happened after the user saw the trigger. 245 people who saw the modal were already paying beforehand — correctly excluded from the 123.
  • Click isn't required to convert: only 55 of the 123 buyers fired the "Upgrade to Pro" click event; the other 68 paid after seeing the modal without that click registering (known tab-navigation gap).
  • Don't rank the 5 features by their conversion rate — with only 8 buyers spread across them, each rate is just 0–3 people. The reliable figure is the total (~1–2%). Re-run over 30 days for trustworthy per-feature rates.
  • "Paid" ≠ all subscriptions: some buyers purchased a one-off (Document Export / GPT3 Credits), not a plan.
